About

I am a Postdoctoral Research Associate at the School of Biology and Environment Science, Queensland University of Technology,

Ecosystem services · Trade

International trade mediating nature's contributions to people

Integrated trade models and Nature's Contributions to People (NCP) framework to quantify how global commodity trade transfers ecosystem service burdens across national boundaries. Subnational analysis revealed that 77–89% of NCP variance lies within countries rather than between them — challenging country-level aggregation common in global assessments.

WILDERNESS FUTURES · GLOBAL

Climate change threatens wilderness faster than we think

Earth's remaining wilderness areas, our last refuges of intact biodiversity, are racing against a climate clock. This research quantified how quickly novel climate conditions are projected to arrive across global wilderness, finding that under high emissions, most wilderness faces climatic disruption within just 25 years. Even under optimistic scenarios, 74–84% of wilderness areas will experience climate shift within a century; well within the timeframe of current international biodiversity commitments. These findings reveal a critical mismatch: conservation targets set for 2050 are being made for landscapes that will already be climatically transformed by the time those targets come due. Protecting wilderness on paper is not enough — climate action and conservation policy must move together.
